# Landing Page Design Guidelines

## Overview

A landing page has one job: convert visitors into leads or customers. It must be focused, clear, and user-driven. Every element should guide the user toward a single goal.

## Landing Page Types

| Type | Purpose | Structure |
|------|---------|-----------|
| **Click-through** | Prep visitors before a big commitment | Light on friction, heavy on benefits |
| **Lead-generation** | Capture contact info | Entire page funnels toward a form |
| **SaaS product** | Explain product, show it in action, handle objections | Multi-section, benefit-focused, conversion-optimized |
| **E-commerce** | Sell a product | High-quality imagery, clear pricing, reviews, frictionless checkout |
| **Agency/service** | Sell expertise and trust | Case studies, process breakdowns, team credibility |
| **Event/webinar/waitlist** | Create urgency | Limited spots, countdown timers, speaker lineups, simple signup |

## Anatomy of a High-Converting Landing Page

### Hero Section (Above the Fold)

This is your three-second test. Visitors must immediately understand what you do and why they should care.

**Required elements:**
- Clear headline that communicates value (not just features)
- Supporting subheadline that adds context
- Primary CTA that is impossible to miss
- Visual that shows the product or concept in action

**Landing page value proposition:** State it plainly. "We help X do Y" beats clever wordplay.

**Benefits vs. features:** Lead with benefits, support with features. People buy outcomes, not specifications.

### Social Proof

Apply social proof to build trust and credibility:
- **Testimonials, ratings, and reviews**
- **Client logos** (recognizable ones)
- **Usage stats** (e.g., "trusted by 10,000+ developers")
- **Case study snippets** or press mentions

**Placement advice:**
- Include smaller chunks in the hero (e.g., logos of clients)
- Add more detailed social proofs later in the page
- Towards the end: consider adding a testimonial wall

### The Rest of the Page

The role of the rest of the page is to convince users to take action. It lets users explore more, eliminates doubts, and guides towards the desired outcome.

**Key sections:**
1. **Building trust:** Show credibility (real person/organization, up-to-date content, transparent options)
2. **Showing benefits:** Instead of "Here's what our product can do," show "Here's what you can do with our product." Show solutions in context (short videos, product demos, screenshots, interactive elements)
3. **Providing details:** Anticipate and answer questions (How does this work? Is this for me? Why act now?)
4. **Telling the story:** Team, values, mission — create an emotional connection and differentiate the brand
5. **Presenting options:** Limit the number of options (Hick's law). Visually highlight one recommended option (Von Restorff effect). Add reassurances (e.g., "Cancel anytime")

## CTA Best Practices

- **Placement:** Primary CTA should appear at least 2–3 times on a long page: hero, mid-page, and footer.
- **Button copy:** Make it high-contrast and descriptive. "Start free trial" beats "Submit." "Book a demo" beats "Learn more."
- **Microcopy:** "Start building for free" is more compelling than "Sign up."
- **Secondary CTAs:** Include fallback options for users not ready to convert (e.g., download free resources, subscribe to newsletter, "Remind me later")

## Form Design

- Only ask for what you absolutely need. Every field you add kills conversion.
- Email alone is often enough. If you need more, explain why.
- Use helpful error messages and clear labels.
- Simplify forms to increase completion rates.

## FAQ & Objection Handling

- Answer the questions holding people back: pricing concerns, security, integrations, commitment level.
- Include a FAQ section if users expect to find it (Jakob's law).
- Address privacy concerns and legal questions proactively.

## Visual Hierarchy

- Use larger fonts for headlines.
- Highlight key information with contrast.
- Position important elements above the fold.
- Use whitespace to create breathing room.
- Make the page scannable with short paragraphs, bullet points, and clear section breaks.

## Performance Optimization

- **Core Web Vitals:**
  - Largest Contentful Paint (LCP): under 2.5 seconds
  - First Input Delay (FID): under 100ms
  - Cumulative Layout Shift (CLS): under 0.1
- **Speed improvements:**
  - Compress images (use WebP format)
  - Lazy load anything below the fold
  - Minimize JavaScript bloat
  - Use a CDN for static assets
  - Optimize hosting

## Responsive & Mobile-First Design

- Over 60% of web traffic is mobile.
- Buttons must be big enough to tap.
- Typography should be readable without zooming.
- Layouts should reflow logically.
- Images should load fast on slow connections.

## Accessibility

- Use sufficient color contrast (WCAG AA minimum).
- Provide alt text for images.
- Ensure keyboard navigation works.
- Create logical heading hierarchy.
- Test with screen readers.

## Copywriting & Psychology

- **Benefit-driven headlines:** Start with the outcome, not the process.
- **Clarity beats cleverness:** If someone has to re-read your headline, you've lost them.
- **Trust signals:** "No credit card required," "Cancel anytime," "14-day money-back guarantee."
- **Persuasion levers:** Urgency, scarcity, social proof, authority — only when genuine.
- **Reciprocity:** Give something valuable before requesting data or money.

## Testing & Optimization

- **Track metrics:** Conversion rate, scroll depth, click heatmaps, form abandonment.
- **Run small tests:** A/B test the headline, then the hero visual, then the CTA. Tiny changes can have big impact.
- **Fix friction ruthlessly:** High mobile bounce = broken layout. People scroll but don't click = weak CTA. Nobody reaches FAQ = wrong placement.
- **Iterate every 2–4 weeks:** Landing pages are living assets, not "set and forget."
